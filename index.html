About Lifeline

Lifeline is a national charity providing all Australians experiencing emotional distress with access to 24-hour crisis support and suicide prevention services. They are committed to empowering Australians to be suicide-safe through connection, compassion, and hope.

Their services are available to anyone, at any time, and for any reason.

Key Services Offered

  • 24/7 Telephone Crisis Support: Call 13 11 14.
  • Online Crisis Chat: Confidential one-on-one chat with a Crisis Supporter (visit their website for hours).
  • Text Message Crisis Support: Confidential text conversation with a Crisis Supporter (visit their website for hours).
  • Community Services: Various programs and services supporting mental health and wellbeing in communities.
  • Resources & Information: Fact sheets, toolkits, and information on mental health topics.
  • Suicide Prevention Programs: Initiatives aimed at reducing Australia's suicide rate.

When to Contact Lifeline

You can contact Lifeline if you are:

  • Feeling overwhelmed, distressed, or anxious.
  • Having suicidal thoughts.
  • Worried about someone else.
  • Experiencing loneliness or isolation.
  • Going through a difficult time (e.g., relationship issues, grief, financial stress).
  • Needing to talk to someone without judgment.

How You Can Support Lifeline

Lifeline relies on community support to continue its vital work. You can help by:

  • Donating: Every dollar helps save lives.
  • Volunteering: Become a Crisis Supporter or help in other ways.
  • Fundraising: Host an event or participate in a challenge.
  • Advocating: Raise awareness about mental health and Lifeline's services.
