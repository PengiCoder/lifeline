About Lifeline Australia

Lifeline is a national charity dedicated to providing all Australians experiencing emotional distress with access to 24-hour crisis support and suicide prevention services. With a vision of an Australia free of suicide, Lifeline works to empower individuals and communities to be suicide-safe through connection, compassion, and hope.

Their services are confidential, non-judgmental, and available to anyone, at any time, for any reason. They believe that no one should have to face their darkest moments alone.

How Lifeline Helps

24/7 Telephone Crisis Support

Confidential phone support by trained Crisis Supporters. Call 13 11 14 anytime.

Online Crisis Chat

One-on-one confidential chat with a Crisis Supporter. Ideal if you prefer typing. (Check official site for hours).

Text Message Support

Confidential text conversation when you need support on the go. (Check official site for hours).

Community Programs

Various programs that support mental health, suicide prevention education, and community wellbeing.

Information & Resources

Provides fact sheets, toolkits, and valuable information on a range of mental health topics and self-care.

Support for Worried Friends/Family

If you're concerned about someone, Lifeline can provide guidance on how to support them.

When to Reach Out

You don't have to be suicidal to call Lifeline. They are there for anyone struggling with:

  • Overwhelming emotions
  • Anxiety or depression
  • Thoughts of self-harm
  • Loneliness or isolation
  • Relationship difficulties
  • Grief and loss
  • Financial stress
  • Or just needing to talk
